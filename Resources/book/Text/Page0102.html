In Hebrew, kinsman-redeemer is go’el. That title describes a role given by the law in Leviticus to a man who would help out a family member in distress by “redeeming” them. The law of Israel declared that a kinsman-redeemer was responsible to redeem a relative who had fallen on hard times and needed rescue. This was called the Levirate law.
