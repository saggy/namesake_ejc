Think of a person from your childhood or more recent past who made you feel bad about yourself. What words pop into your mind that this person might use about you?

The truth is that our internal nametags are filled with both kinds of names: those that we have been given by people who shower us with encouragement and admiration, and those that have been given in careless moments of criticism. The reminder that we are also accountable for naming the people we love with our words is a sobering and sacred responsibility.
