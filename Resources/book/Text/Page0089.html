What do we learn about Ruth’s work ethic in Ruth 2:7?

When they arrived in Bethlehem, Ruth found herself in a desperate situation. In that particular time and culture, the male members of the household would work to supply food and shelter for their family. Ruth and Naomi had no one to provide for them, a situation that made their grief and loss seem even more hopeless. If no one took action on their behalf, it was possible that they could starve or be forced to beg on the streets.

Instead of wallowing or waiting for someone else to take responsibility, Ruth channeled her grief, worry, and anxiety into action. With Naomi’s blessing, she went to the fields each day to glean—to gather the leftovers the harvesters had dropped. She worked tirelessly until sundown, and we’re told she worked the entire season until the harvest had finished. Ruth’s initiative and determination saved her life and Naomi’s, and it set into motion a course of events that would change their lives forever.

Read Isaiah 6:8. God is calling for someone to step up and take action. What is Isaiah’s response? Can you hear Ruth making a similar declaration?

When you’ve been faced with circumstances that overwhelmed you, how did you respond? Do you identify more with Naomi’s reaction of being rendered inactive, frozen by her circumstances, or Ruth’s “get-up-and-go” tendency to take care of it herself?
