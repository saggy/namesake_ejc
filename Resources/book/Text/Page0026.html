Third, and best of all, God promised them a presence—His presence.

Reread Genesis 17:7-8 and fill in the blanks below:

He promised to be with them and to be their God—to love and take care of them.

God promised Abraham and Sarah three things: the two greatest desires of their hearts—people and place—and a third thing they hadn’t even imagined they could desire, because the presence of a God who loved and cared and guided them through life was unheard of. The greatest promise of all was that He would be with them, establishing a covenant to be their God and the God of their descendants. This third promise was worth more than the others combined—God’s presence with them. The greatest promise of all is God’s presence, His commitment to walk with us as His children.
